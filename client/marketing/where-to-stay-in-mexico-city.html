FAQ

Common questions

What is the best neighborhood to stay in Mexico City for first-time visitors?

Condesa and Roma Norte are the default first-timer picks: leafy, walkable, and full of cafés. Polanco suits luxury and museums; Centro Histórico suits maximum sightseeing.

Is Condesa safe and walkable for tourists?

Condesa is one of CDMX's most walkable visitor districts — parks, restaurants, and galleries in a compact radius. Always use normal big-city awareness at night.

Condesa or Polanco — which is better?

Condesa is leafier and café-led; Polanco is upscale with museums and fine dining. See our Condesa vs Polanco guide, then match hotels by room photos.

Can I search Mexico City hotels by room photos?

Yes. TravelByVibe is built for CDMX with thousands of indexed hotels. Describe rainfall shower, bright suite, or design-forward room and browse real matches.

Is Centro Histórico good for hotels?

Centro is unbeatable for Zócalo and Templo Mayor access but busier and louder. Choose it when culture density beats quiet evenings.
